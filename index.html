01 / The differenceXIM · in-memory compute
The difference

Compute where the data lives

Conventional accelerators inherit the von Neumann bottleneck. They burn most of their energy shuttling weights back and forth to the compute units. We keep the weights in SRAM and do the work right where they sit.

Ximplic in-memory

Compute in SRAM
✓Weights stay resident inside the compute array
✓Inference runs in place, with no data shuttling
✓Energy scales with useful work, not memory traffic
✓Standard SRAM you can build today, sized to your budget

Conventional NPU

von Neumann
✕Weights streamed from memory every inference
✕Data shuttled back and forth to the compute units
✕Memory traffic dominates total energy
✕Battery drains fast on small, frequently-run models
