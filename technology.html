How it works, in plain words

Devices that listen, watch and sense all day need AI that barely touches the battery. Here is the idea behind ours, with no jargon.

The problem

Most of the battery is wasted just carrying information

In a normal AI chip, the part that remembers things and the part that does the maths sit apart. For every small decision, information is carried back and forth between them. That carrying, not the thinking, is what flattens the battery.

The Ximplic way

Think in place
✓The memory does the maths itself
✓Almost nothing is carried around
✓The battery lasts far longer
✓Built from standard, proven memory

A normal AI chip

Today

The idea

Do the thinking right where the information is kept

Picture a chef who keeps every ingredient on the counter instead of running to the pantry for each one. We do the same for AI: the memory itself does the work, so information barely has to move.

  • The memory and the calculator become one
  • Information is read and used in the same place
  • Far less movement, so far less power
  • Made from standard memory, so it can be built anywhere
Information goes in, the memory works out the answer in place, and only the answer comes out.
Why it matters

Less carrying means a lot less power

The actual maths costs about the same either way. The big difference is all the carrying around it, which our approach mostly removes.
