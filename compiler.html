Bring your own model. The XIM Compiler maps your trained network onto the in-memory inference accelerator, targeting both the Virtual Prototype and the RTL, so the numbers you measure in software are the numbers you ship in silicon.

01 / What it doesXIM Compiler

From your model to the accelerator

A single flow that takes a standard model and prepares it to run efficiently on the XIM in-memory engine.

Ingest your model

Start from your ONNX export or framework model, no need to hand-rewrite networks for the accelerator.

Prepare for in-memory

The compiler quantises and schedules your network for the XIM compute array across INT8–INT1 precision.

Target VP and RTL

The same compiled graph runs on the Virtual Prototype and the RTL IP, consistent results from software to silicon.
