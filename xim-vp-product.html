01 / What it isXIM Virtual Prototype

Design and ship around the XIM accelerator before the silicon exists

In-memory computing collapses the memory wall for always-on edge AI, but integrating a CIM accelerator into a real SoC is hard to evaluate on paper. The XIM Virtual Prototype is an executable, cycle-approximate model of the accelerator IP: drop it into your SystemC platform, run your own neural workloads, and get trustworthy cycles, latency and energy numbers to drive architecture, software, and partnership decisions.

The XIM DIMC accelerator keeps weights resident inside the SRAM compute array and runs inference in place, eliminating the weight-streaming energy that dominates conventional NPUs on small, frequently-run models. It targets the workloads that run continuously on battery: keyword spotting, anomaly detection, sensor fusion, and compact vision.

The Virtual Prototype lets your team de-risk that integration today: measure end-to-end inference latency and energy on your models, validate the host/driver software path, and explore voltage/frequency and clustering trade-offs, all in pure software, with no FPGA board and no proprietary EDA tools required.

Probe the accelerator from your testbench

A stable C++ probe API exposes the numbers that matter, per inference:

// link libxim_component.a into your SoC VP auto xim = new xim_accelerator_tlm("xim", /*clusters*/4); xim->set_operating_point(0.6, 250.0); // V, MHz // ...run your command stream over AXI... xim->get_cycle_count(); // wall-clock cycles xim->get_compute_latency_ns(); // latency xim->get_energy_pJ(); // dynamic + leakage xim->get_compute_tops_per_watt();
02 / CapabilitiesXIM Virtual Prototype

Everything you need to evaluate and integrate

Configurable compute array

A configurable in-memory compute array with INT8 / INT4 / INT2 / INT1 precision, explore the accelerator size and operating point that fit your power and area budget.

RTL-correlated timing

The compute-cycle model is correlated to RTL co-simulation (R² = 1.0000 on the INT8/INT4 dense path) and board-calibrated end-to-end on DS-CNN-L to within ±5% per layer.

Standard SoC interfaces

ARM AMBA AXI4 + AXI4-Lite (TLM-2.0 kit verified), an IEEE 1685-2014 IP-XACT register map as the single source of truth, and a memory-mapped command/IRQ model.

Energy & DVFS modeling

Dynamic and leakage energy with power-gating and voltage/frequency sweeps, quantify TOPS/W and battery life across operating points for your workloads.

Drop-in integration

An integration shim template, a RISC-V reference SoC, a standalone benchmarking harness, and 8 callback hooks, wire the IP into your platform in days, not weeks.

Your workloads, measured

A workload runner with 20+ MAC-honest model builders, MLPerf Tiny, MobileNet/ResNet/EfficientNet, transformers and LLMs, plus HTML/CSV reports and cross-model comparison.

Questions

Frequently asked

What exactly do I get to evaluate?+

The XIM Virtual Prototype, a SystemC TLM-2.0 model of the accelerator you plug into your own SoC platform over AXI, run your own models, and measure latency and energy. No FPGA board, no proprietary EDA, zero licence cost to evaluate.

Do I need a special toolchain or FPGA board?+

No. The Virtual Prototype runs in pure software on open SystemC. You evaluate entirely on your own machines before committing to any hardware.

Which models can it run?+

Bring your own. The XIM Compiler maps your ONNX or framework model onto the accelerator and targets both the Virtual Prototype and the RTL, so what you measure in software is what you ship in silicon.

How is the performance validated?+

The model is correlated to RTL co-simulation and calibrated against silicon-board measurement. We're transparent about what's measured versus projected, and share the full methodology under NDA.

How do we move from evaluation to silicon?+

Evaluate with the Virtual Prototype, then license the RTL IP and design it into your SoC with our support, the same validated design, software to silicon.
