What it is

Design around the accelerator before the silicon exists

An executable, cycle-approximate model of the accelerator IP. Drop it into your SystemC platform, run your own workloads, and get trustworthy cycles, latency and energy to drive your architecture and software.

The accelerator keeps weights resident in the SRAM array and computes in place. It targets the workloads that run continuously on battery: keyword spotting, anomaly detection, sensor fusion and compact vision.

De-risk the integration today. Measure latency and energy on your models, validate the driver path, and explore operating points, all in software with no FPGA board and no proprietary EDA.

Probe the accelerator from your testbench

A stable C++ probe API exposes the numbers that matter, per inference:

// link libxim_component.a into your SoC VP auto xim = new xim_accelerator_tlm("xim", /*clusters*/4); xim->set_operating_point(0.6, 250.0); // V, MHz // ...run your command stream over AXI... xim->get_cycle_count(); // wall-clock cycles xim->get_compute_latency_ns(); // latency xim->get_energy_pJ(); // dynamic + leakage xim->get_compute_tops_per_watt();
Capabilities

Everything you need to evaluate and integrate

Configurable compute array

A configurable compute array with INT8 / INT4 / INT2 / INT1 precision. Size it to your power and area budget.

RTL-correlated timing

The compute-cycle model is correlated to RTL co-simulation (R² = 1.0000 on the INT8/INT4 dense path) and FPGA-board-calibrated end-to-end on DS-CNN-L to within ±5% per layer.

Energy & DVFS modeling

Dynamic and leakage energy with power-gating and DVFS sweeps. Quantify TOPS/W and battery life across operating points.

Your workloads, measured

A workload runner with 20+ MAC-honest model builders (MLPerf Tiny, MobileNet, ResNet, transformers, LLMs), plus HTML/CSV reports and cross-model comparison.
